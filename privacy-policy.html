Section 01

Information We Collect

We collect information in the following ways:

Information you provide directly:

  • Full name, date of birth, gender identity, and contact information (phone, email, address)
  • Insurance information and billing details
  • Medical and psychiatric history, current symptoms, medications, and treatment history
  • Emergency contact information
  • Information submitted through intake forms, appointment requests, or secure messages

Information collected automatically:

  • IP address, browser type, and device information when you visit our website
  • Pages visited, time spent on site, and referring URLs
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies (see Section 6)

Information from third parties:

  • Prior medical records obtained with your written authorization
  • Insurance eligibility and claims information from payers
  • Referral information from other healthcare providers
Section 02

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide, coordinate, and manage your psychiatric care and treatment
  • Schedule, confirm, and manage appointments
  • Process billing, insurance claims, and payments
  • Send appointment reminders, care instructions, and clinical communications
  • Comply with applicable legal and regulatory obligations
  • Improve the quality and safety of our services

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal or health information to third parties for marketing or commercial purposes — ever.

Section 03

HIPAA & Protected Health Information

As a healthcare provider, Mindful Methods is a Covered Entity under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). Your medical and mental health records are considered Protected Health Information (PHI) and are governed by strict federal privacy and security rules.

Under HIPAA, we are permitted to use and disclose your PHI for the following purposes without your prior written authorization:

  • Treatment: To provide, coordinate, or manage your healthcare and related services
  • Payment: To obtain reimbursement for services rendered, including billing your insurance
  • Healthcare Operations: For activities such as quality improvement, training, and administrative functions

For all other uses and disclosures of your PHI — including sharing with family members, employers, or for marketing purposes — we will obtain your written authorization prior to disclosure. You have the right to revoke that authorization at any time.

A complete description of our HIPAA practices is available in our Notice of Privacy Practices (NPP), which you will receive upon becoming a patient and which is available upon request at hello@mindfulmethodsinc.com.

Section 04

Disclosure of Information

We may share your information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Business Associates: Third-party vendors who perform services on our behalf (such as electronic health record platforms, billing services, and scheduling tools) under signed Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) that require them to protect your information in accordance with HIPAA
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law, court order, or government regulation
  • Mandatory Reporting: As required by California law, including mandatory reporting of suspected child abuse, elder abuse, or imminent danger to self or others
  • Emergency Situations: To prevent or lessen a serious and imminent threat to health or safety
  • With Your Authorization: To any other party with your explicit written consent

Mental Health Records: California law provides additional protections for mental health records beyond HIPAA requirements. Psychotherapy notes are afforded the highest level of protection and require separate, specific authorization for release.

Section 05

Telehealth & Technology

Mindful Methods delivers psychiatric services exclusively via telehealth. We use HIPAA-compliant platforms for all video sessions and electronic communications. By using our telehealth services, you acknowledge:

  • Sessions are conducted over encrypted, HIPAA-compliant video technology
  • You are responsible for ensuring your own privacy during sessions (choosing a private location, using headphones if needed)
  • Sessions are never recorded without your explicit written consent
  • Electronic communications sent through our patient portal are encrypted and secure
  • Standard email and text messaging are not fully secure — please use the patient portal for sensitive communications

Our scheduling and intake platform (IntakeQ/PracticeQ) and booking platform (Headway) operate under their own privacy policies and are bound by BAAs with our practice.

Section 06

Cookies & Website Tracking

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to improve your browsing experience. Cookies are small text files stored on your device. We use:

  • Essential cookies: Required for basic website functionality (e.g., navigation, session management)
  • Analytics cookies: To understand how visitors use our site so we can improve it (e.g., Google Analytics). This data is anonymized and aggregated.
  • Preference cookies: To remember your settings and preferences

You may disable cookies through your browser settings; however, doing so may limit certain website functionality. We do not use cookies to track your health information or link website behavior to your clinical records.

Section 07

Your Rights

Under HIPAA and applicable California law, you have the following rights regarding your health information:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of your medical records and health information
  • Right to Amend: Request corrections to inaccurate or incomplete records
  • Right to Restrict: Request restrictions on certain uses or disclosures of your PHI
  • Right to an Accounting: Receive a list of disclosures of your PHI made in the past six years
  • Right to a Notice: Receive our Notice of Privacy Practices
  • Right to File a Complaint: File a complaint with us or with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services if you believe your privacy rights have been violated — without retaliation
  • Right to Confidential Communications: Request that we communicate with you in a specific way or at a specific location

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at hello@mindfulmethodsinc.com. We will respond within 30 days.

Section 08

Minors

Our website is not directed to individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from minors through our website without parental or guardian consent. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately.

For minors receiving clinical services, we comply with all applicable California laws governing the privacy rights of minors in the context of mental health treatment, including provisions that allow certain minors to consent to outpatient mental health services without parental authorization.

Section 09

Data Security

We implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect your information against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction, consistent with HIPAA Security Rule requirements. These include:

  • Encrypted data transmission (TLS/SSL) across all platforms
  • Secure, password-protected electronic health record systems
  • Access controls limiting PHI access to authorized personnel only
  • Regular security risk assessments
  • Business Associate Agreements with all third-party vendors

In the event of a breach of unsecured PHI, we will notify affected individuals as required by the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule and applicable California law.

No method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. If you have concerns, please contact us directly.

Section 10

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, notify active patients by email.

Your continued use of our website or services after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy. We encourage you to review this page periodically.
